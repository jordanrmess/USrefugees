Introduction:¶

For this project, we wanted to gain insight into refugees. A refugee is defined as an individual who has been forced to leave their country in order to escape war, persecution, or natural disaster. Recently, there has been a lot of discussion over America's refugee policy as well as the Syrian refugee crisis that has had global effects. And while we had heard a lot about refugees through the news, we had a lot of questions remaining: how many refugees enter countries such as the U.S. each year and how has this amount changed over the past few decades? Historically, what different countries do refugees come from and what age are these refugees? How does country of origin and country of entrance affect whether or not a refugee applicant is accepted or rejected? And finally, what different factors could we use to predict whether or not a refugee is accepted?

The following tutorial will outline how we followed the data lifecycle to gain insight into the questions above as well as come up with a final message based on the insight we gained. For the first three parts we will show how to gather and clean up data, create data visualizations that specifically focus on the questions given above, and finally gather conclusions from these visualizations. Then in part four, we will use machine learning to provide final analysis based on the information we gathered from the first three parts and conclude with a final message.
